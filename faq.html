Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about credentialing

How long does credentialing typically take?

Credentialing with most insurance panels takes 60-120 days from application submission. Our team works to expedite the process by ensuring all documents are complete and following up regularly with payers. We've reduced average turnaround time to just 45 days.

What documents do I need to provide?

Typical requirements include: current state license, DEA certificate, board certification, malpractice insurance certificate, CV/resume, medical diplomas, government-issued ID, and work history. We provide a detailed checklist after consultation.

Do you work with all provider types?

Yes! We credential physicians (MD/DO), nurse practitioners (NP), physician assistants (PA), psychologists, physical therapists, occupational therapists, speech therapists, dentists, and other healthcare professionals.

What is CAQH and why is it important?

CAQH (Council for Affordable Quality Healthcare) is a centralized credentialing database used by most insurance companies. Keeping your CAQH profile current and attested quarterly is essential for timely reimbursements. We handle this entirely for you.

How much do your services cost?

Pricing varies based on number of providers and insurance panels. We offer transparent, competitive pricing with no hidden fees. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your practice needs.

Do you offer ongoing support after credentialing?

Yes! We offer maintenance packages that include CAQH attestation reminders, revalidation services, adding new insurance panels as your practice grows, and ongoing compliance monitoring.

Which insurance panels do you work with?

We work with Medicare, Medicaid, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, Aetna, UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and 50+ commercial insurance panels nationwide.

Is my information secure with Quad Solutions?

Absolutely. We are HIPAA compliant and use bank-level encryption for all sensitive data. Your information is never shared with third parties without your consent.
